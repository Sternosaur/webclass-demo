so I joined the army instead. After 2 deployments to Afghanistan that was enough for me

to Arizona to catch up with some family who moved out from Chicago years ago where I grew.

I figured if I had to get a degree in anything, I wanted it to be game related since that
was my biggest passion. Unfortunately there weren't any great game design colleges in
Phoenix, so I ended up moving back to Chicago to persue a degree in game design.
Now I'm finally here for my last semester. I have decided to make a lot of drastic
changes to my life this year. I got married, lost 50 pounds, got a house, and now I'm
learning how to make a website and starting to build out a live streaming/gaming setup
in my basement.

I don't want to make it sound like game design is just a plan B for me
or something, but I feel like I owe it to myself to at least try to create a set schedule
for streaming and making videos between Twitch and YouTube and give it the college try to
follow my dreams for a couple of years. I've got a lot going on right now though between
full time college and work, but I'm at least getting some footage recording some fun
sessions with my friends in the meantime. I'd rather try for a couple of years and fail
then to never give it a try and always wonder what if.


Wish me luck!
